THE PEONY

The peony comes in every colour except blue, and can live and bloom for 100 years.

Peonies do best if planted in the fall. Plant in loamy, moist, well draining soil with neutral PH. Plant in full sunlight. Dig a hole about 2 feet across and 2 feet deep. Set the root so the eyes face up and are no more than 1.5 to 2 inches below soil line. Backfill with soil.

Water well.

THE ROSE
There are over 100 species of the rose.

Roses need 5 - 6 hours of sunlight and to be out of the wind. Prepare a hole twice as wide, and 8 inches deeper than the container. Partially refill the hole ensuring that the bud union is at soil level. Fill with water and let drain.Refill the rest of the hole once the water has drained. Pack the soil with your foot, leaving a slight depression.

Water well.

THE HYDRANGEA

There are about 75 species of hydrangea. The colors of the flowers are affected by the aluminum ions in the soil.

Hydrangea prefer rich, porous moist soil. Full morning sun with some afternoon shade. Dig the hole as deep as the root ball and 2 to 3 times as wide. Set plant in and fill hole with water. When water has drained fill in the rest of the hole.

Water well.

FOXGLOVE

Foxglove produces only leaves during the first year of life. Flowering stem, flowers and seed are produced during the second year.

Set plants out in spring or fall, spacing plants 1 to 2 feet apart, depending on the variety; sow seeds in early summer. Break up the soil to a depth of 12 to 15 inches, then mix in a 2- to 4-inch layer of compost. Dig a hole twice the diameter of the pot the plant is in. place it in the hole so the top of the root ball is level with the soil surface. Carefully fill in around the root ball and firm the soil gently.

Water thoroughly.

THE TULIP

Currently, there are around 75 wild species of tulips and 150 species in total with over 3000 varieties.

Plant tulip bulbs in the fall. They prefer full or afternoon sun and well drained soil. You should space bulbs 4 to 6" apart and set 8" deep. Cover and press soil firmly.

Water well.

LAVENDER

There are around 75 wild species of tulips and 150 species in total with over 3000 varieties.

You should choose a sunny and well drained location. Plant 2 months before first hard frost. Dig hole - loosen roots and plant.

Water tulips during dry spells in the fall; otherwise, do not water.

DELPHINIUM

There are over 400 species of Delphiniums, and have small black seeds.

Plant in well drained soil in full sun to light shade and shelter from wind. Plant them in the spring. Amend the soil with compost. Dig a hole twice the diameter of the container and ensure that the top off the root ball is level with the soil. Fill with soil and water.

Water them regularly and thoroughly, but do not let water pool around the crown of the plant.

LILIES

Only white and tiger lilies are known by their beautiful smell. Other species of lily are odorless.

Plant bulbs in fall. Lillie's prefer 6 to 8 hours of sunlight. The soil should be loosened 12 to 15" down. Make sure to grow them in rich soil. Dig hole 2 to 3 times as deep as the bulbs are high and set in hole with pointy side up. Fill the hole with soil and tamp gently.

Water thoroughly.

ALLIUM

They can grow 6 inches to 3 feet tall and attract birds.

Make sure to plant in well drained soil in full sunlight. Dig the holes and plant them 3" deep and 6 to 8" apart. Plant the pointy side up. Fill holes and cover with soil

Water thoroughly.
